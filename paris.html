Bonjour Paris ....

Places to see

Eiffel Tower

This is one of Paris' most popular attractions with, on average, 7 million visitors every year. It was designed and built by a french civil engineer named Gustave Eiffel, for the Exposition Universelle, which celebrated the 100 year anniversary of the French Revolution. Its construction began in 1887 and eneded in 1889. The structure is made of iron and has a height of around 330 metres which made it the tallest human-made structure in the world, between 1889 to 1930. The Eiffel Tower has three levels. There are lovely resturants on levels one and two, however we would reccomend you take a trip up to level three for some incredible views across Paris. Ticket prices range between 18,10€ to 28,30€ for adults and 2,80€ to 7,10€ for children. The Eiffel Tower also lights up at every night at 9pm. Definitely worth a visit!

Louvre Museum

The Louvre is a national art museum, located on the right Bank of the Seine. It is home to some of the world's most iconic pieces, including the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo. The museum is housed in the Louvre Palace, originally built in the late 12th to 13th century under Philip II. It is not possible to see all of the museum in one visit so make sure to plan out what you would like to see, or join a guided tour instead. We would reccomend that you purchase tickets online to avoid the large queues at the museum. Tickets cost €17 online and €15 at the museum.

Notre Dame

The Notre Dame is a medieval Catholic cathedral, located on the eastern half of Île de la Cité, an island in the Seine River. It was constructed between the 12th and 14th centuries. In the early 21st century, approximately 12 million people visited Notre-Dame annually, making it the most visited monument in Paris. The cathedral is known for its French Gothic architecture and houses many religious and historical artifacts. During its renovation in 2019, the roof of the cathedral caught fire and burnt for a total of 15 hours. The cathedral is now under restoration and is set to reopen on 8th December 2024.

Sacre Coeur

The Scare Coeur, also known as Sacré-Cœur Basilica, is a Roman Catholic church, located at the summit of the butte of Montmartre. It is the second most popular tourist destination in the capital after the Eiffel Tower. From the Sacre Coeur, there are spectacular views all over Paris. The basilica was first proposed by Felix Fournier, the Bishop of Nantes, in 1870 after the defeat of France and the capture of Napoleon III at the Battle of Sedan in the Franco-Prussian War. Construction began in 1875 and continued for forty years under five different architects. It was later completed in 1914. The area of Montmartre also has some lovely cafes and shops you could visit.
